# Welcome by MC

**Adam Wiggins · Local-First Conf 2026 · 2026-07-12**

[Official schedule](https://app-2026.localfirstconf.com/schedule/day-1/0915-welcome-by-mc) · [Raw transcript](../raw/2026-07-12%20-%20Adam%20Wiggins%20-%20Welcome%20by%20MC.md) · [Cleaned transcript](../cleaned/2026-07-12%20-%20Adam%20Wiggins%20-%20Welcome%20by%20MC.md)

## Overview

Adam Wiggins welcomes attendees by framing Local-First Conf 2026 around user empowerment and values in turbulent times, previews a program that extends familiar local-first subjects into open social systems, local AI, games, spreadsheets, and calendars, and explains the conference logistics before introducing the first speaker, Martin Kleppmann.

## Detailed notes

Wiggins opens by directing attendees to the online schedule and its QR codes. He describes the program as a widening of the umbrella around user empowerment: alongside established topics such as data, real-time collaboration, sync engines, and CRDTs, the conference will cover open social systems, local and open-weight models, agent harnesses, AI-adjacent work intended to align with the community's values, video games, spreadsheets, and calendars.

He says this breadth is one reason he is excited about the year's talks. Against what he calls turbulent times, he argues that values provide a foundation to return to while the world changes.

Wiggins then covers logistics. The conference is single-track and remains in the same room for all three days. The first two days follow a conventional speaker format, while day three is a lab day: the chairs will be removed for a more open hacking-conference format that he compares loosely to a music festival. Lunch is at 12:30, with two breaks during the day and coffee available at the back of the room.

Because the schedule is tight, Wiggins says the organizers will keep transitions and Q&A brief. Rather than passing microphones through the audience, attendees should submit questions in Discord near the end of each talk; he will select a few for speakers during the A/V switchover. He also announces a closing party at a nearby beer garden on day three, noting that details are in the schedule.

After asking how the audience is feeling, Wiggins introduces Martin Kleppmann as an associate professor at the University of Cambridge, the author of *Designing Data-Intensive Applications*—which he says has sold more than 300,000 copies—and the person who coined the term “local-first.” Kleppmann thanks Wiggins and takes over for the first talk.
